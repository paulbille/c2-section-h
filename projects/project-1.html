Project 1

Intx w/ Type
...

Objectives
...

  • To build a multi-page website 
  • To experiment with navigation to convey meaning
  • To interpret a story through expressive typography online

Introduction
...

Jorge Luis Borges was an Argentinian writer famous for his short stories that deal with labyrinths, dreams, religion, and mathematical ideas (particularly set theory concepts like infinity and cardinality). His circuitous and meandering prose, full of allusions and vivid imagery, is a good way to think about the web as a network that has many nodes and many connections that continuously folds upon itself. It is the act of navigating through this maze that brings meaning to the web experience. 

Project 
...

Read the short story selections here. Choose one short story and set the text using HTML and CSS (JS optional) so that the reader will not only be able to read the story but also experience your interpretation of the story.  Use the visual language of the web—hypertext, responsive design, forms, color, divs—to express the meaning of your selected story.

Requirements
...

  • You must use multiple views to convey this experience — it will be viewed on both large and small (mobile) screens. 
  • You may use no more than two typefaces
  • Representational images are not allowed

Considerations
...

  • Point-of-View: What is text? What is writing? How do you see or read this particular text? The most successful projects have 1 point of view that is expressed through a decisive design move (some examples in the books below)
  • Typography: How does the typography enhance your point of view and help you convey meaning? Pay attention to typographic details: special characters, leading, words per line, etc.
  • Links: How do internal/external links enhance the meaning of this text? 
  • Scale of the Page: What happens to your site when the browser window is resized? Is it the same? Is it a different design? Are only certain information accessible at certain sizes?
  • Do not try to illustrate the text. The least successful projects are ones that try to visualize the text literally. 

Schedule
...

WEEK 1
...

TUE 1/23
...

  • In class: Assign Project 1
  • Homework: Pick a Text, read it & write a short response (1-2 pages):
    • What are the main themes of this text?
    • How is it structured?
    • What type of interactive experience might its themes and structure suggest to you as a designer?

THUR 1/25
...

  • In class: Small groups: share your text summary, its main themes & how you're thinking about translating these into a website.
  • Homework: Wireframe three structures for the text. How will it break down into multiple pages & how are these linked together? You can consider styling and functionality but please focus mainly on structure (in other words, "think in HTML").

WEEK 2
...

TUE 1/30
...

  • In class: Small groups: present your wireframe concepts. Offer one another feedback.
  • Homework: Pick one direction of the three you made and flesh out any additional details so that you are ready to code. Then, set up an HTML ONLY document and begin to structure your text in HTML. No CSS or JS is permitted at this stage of the project!

THUR 2/1
...

  • In class: Small groups: share project links & interact with one another's sites. Provide feedback on structure & the experience of navigating through what your classmates have created.
  • Homework: Finish structuring your text in an HTML-only directory with functioning links. These should be published on your gitHub and we should be able to view & interact with them in the browser by class on Tuesday.

WEEK 3
...

TUE 2/6
...

  • Homework: Begin to style your text with CSS.
    • Return to the original themes and structures that you identified.
    • Ask how these can suggest styling possibilites in terms of color, typography, responsivenes and beyond.
    • You should arrive Thursday with with a style sheet added to your repository & pushed so that we can view your styled website in the browser during class.

THUR 2/8
...

  • In class: Small group feedback.
  • Homework: Finish styling your sites and begin to consider functionality (JS).
    • how will your site behave?
    • What types of actions and activites will it enable for its visitors?
    • Return one final time to the questions that you started with:
      • What themes and structures does this text embody?
      • How might these inform an interactive experience with the text?
      • Consider not only of these intital questions but also how your work thus far has transformed the text. Your site now has its own concrete existence to which you are adding a layer of functionality. How can you respond to the work you have already done to draw out its strengths or help us to understand it in new ways?

WEEK 4
...

TUE
...

  • Homework: Continue to work on the functionality of your website. By Thursday you should have either embedded <script> tags or else have linked & pushed a .js to your repository.

THUR
...

  • In class: Small group feedback.
  • Homework: Continue to work on the functionality of your website. Begin to reflect back on all three layers of your site (structure, styling, functionality). Do these feel aligned to one another? Are they each facets of a cohesive & interactive interpretation of your text? Challenge yourself to alter or remove elements if they no longer serve your interpretation.

WEEK 5
...

TUE
...

  • In class: Time to work & get final feedback: your project will be due on Thursday!
  • Homework: Finalize all aspects of your site

THUR
...

  • In class: Final presentations of project one
