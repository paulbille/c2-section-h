Objectives
...

  • To gather and organize and handle a large collection of data 
  • To understand metadata
  • To tell a story through content curation and order
  • To experiment with interactions with a collection
  • To develop a working relationship of word, image, context

Introduction
...

Anything that you have an accumulation of can be a collection. An archive is a collection of historical documents or records. Not all collections are archives, but many become relevant with time. For our next project youre tasked with creating a collection of 50 items minimum. Any time you see a multiplicity of an object, you begin to notice similarities and contexts in a new way. Seeing multiple of an object allows for this kind of in depth research that observing one object does not.

Project
...

Create a collection of minimum 50 items. Your collection should have at least two different data types affiliated with it, and should be as specific as possible. There should be a visual consistency between the items, and should have meta-data/a cohesive story to it. For example: If your collection is My personal History of Cat Memes you might include: photos of the cat memes their name, their year, their author, and a brief description of why each meme matters to you. You might also want to check if a personal archive of cat memes already exists somewhere in the world. Not just because it will help you to contextualize your own but because you might want to not pursue another archive on cats. Unless you are deeply obsessed with cat memes of course.

Once you have your entries collected and collection organized, youll design a webpage that houses the collection. Consider how the collection is organized and experienced one at a time, in a grid, a list, what do they subpages over that the main page does not? Consider if there are groupings within your collection and how the relationship between typography and mixed media can add a visual perspective to your work.

Requirements
...

  • 1 index page
  • 1 css style-sheet that controls all your pages
  • 1 about page, that contextualizes your collection
  • 50 subpages/entries to your collection
    • each entry should have a caption associated with it
    • each entry needs to have meta data included
  • Must be responsive and function on a mobile screen
    • think about a design that works on mobile
    • no fancy js effects that you can't make work on mobile
    • focus on the basics, text, image, structure and their relationship to each other

Considerations
...

CCC (Collecting: Contextualizing Content)
...

  • What are you collecting and why?
  • Why does this collection need to exist?
    • Is it just for you, for someone who wants to learn about this subject?
  • What does your collection offer that cannot be found elsewhere?
  • What kind of different data types can you use to add specificity and interest to your project?
  • How can you tell a story through a curated set of text, visuals, interactive experiences?

Design
...

  • What does the media you collect need?
    • Is it just text and needs a typeface?
    • Is it audio and needs sound?
  • How does the design give context to your collection?
  • How do you present meta-data for each entry?

Access & Navigation
...

  • How can a user access your collection?
  • At night time only?
  • Does your website have opening-hours?
  • Does it have a password?
  • Is it a secret link for select friends?
  • a public github-repository?
  • Can I see the entire collection at once, or only single entries in sequence?
  • Does the arrangement of elements or the grid change?
  • Does the sites navigation change to highlight the most current content?
  • Are there filters or subsections of your data?
